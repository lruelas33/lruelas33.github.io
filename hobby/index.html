BOWLING
Bowling consists of rolling a heavy ceramic ball down a line and
trying to knock down as many pins as possible

How do they score bowling?

When scoring bowling, each pin is worth one point. Bonus points may be earned for throwing a strike (when all the pins are knocked down on the first throw), or a
spare (any remaining pins are knocked down with the second throw). The bonus for a strike is that the total pins of the next two throws are added to the 10 points
scored from the strike. The bonus for a spare is that the pins from the next one throw is added to the 10 points scored from the spare.

What is 10 strikes in a row called?

There is no official term for 10 strikes in a row. If 10 strikes are thrown in a row, the bowler is awarded 2 additional
throws. If both extra throws are strikes, for a game total of 12 strikes in a row, the score will reach 300 points,
which is called a perfect game.

What are the rules of 10 pin bowling?

The rules of 10 pin bowling are to wear special bowling shoes and throw a bowling ball with three drilled holes for fingers down an oiled wooden lane,
in an effort to knock down as many bowling pins as possible. Each player gets 10 turns per game. These turns are called frames. A bowler may throw
the ball two times in each frame, unless they knock down all 10 pins in the first throw of the frame.
